# Strategic Decision Skill - Product Requirement Document

## Overview
- **Summary**: A comprehensive AI skill that provides strategic decision-making support for solo entrepreneurs, including business model design, competitive strategy development, and market analysis frameworks.
- **Purpose**: To empower solo founders with strategic thinking expertise that would typically require a business strategy consultant, enabling them to make informed decisions about their business model, competitive positioning, and market strategy.
- **Target Users**: Solo entrepreneurs, indie hackers, startup founders, and small business owners who need structured strategic guidance.

## Goals
- Provide comprehensive business model design and validation frameworks
- Offer competitive strategy development and positioning templates
- Deliver market analysis methodologies and data interpretation
- Create strategic playbooks with actionable tactics
- Integrate with research integration skill for industry insights

## Non-Goals (Out of Scope)
- Replace professional business strategy consultants entirely
- Provide financial investment advice or accounting services
- Handle real-time market data acquisition or scraping
- Make definitive business decisions on behalf of the user
- Guarantee specific market outcomes or success metrics

## Background & Context
- Strategic decision-making is one of the biggest challenges for solo entrepreneurs
- Limited time and resources require highly efficient, focused strategic thinking
- Multiple business models exist but founders lack expertise in evaluating trade-offs
- Strategy frameworks exist but require adaptation for solo contexts
- This skill bridges the gap by providing comprehensive, AI-powered strategic support

## Functional Requirements

### FR-1: Business Model Design
- Business model canvas and other innovation frameworks
- Revenue model selection and design methodologies
- Value proposition development and testing
- Cost structure and scaling analysis
- Business model innovation techniques

### FR-2: Competitive Strategy
- Competitive landscape mapping methodologies
- Porter's five forces and other analytical frameworks
- Competitive advantage identification and development
- Positioning and differentiation strategies
- Competitive response playbooks

### FR-3: Market Analysis
- Market sizing and TAM/SAM/SOM calculation methodologies
- Customer segmentation and targeting frameworks
- Trend analysis and market opportunity identification
- Customer needs and pain point discovery
- Competitive intelligence gathering guidelines

### FR-4: Strategic Planning
- SWOT analysis and other strategic assessment tools
- Strategic goal setting and OKR frameworks
- Milestone planning and roadmap development
- Scenario planning and risk analysis
- Decision-making frameworks for strategic choices

### FR-5: Execution Playbooks
- Strategy implementation guides and checklists
- Prioritization frameworks for strategic initiatives
- Resource allocation methodologies
- Progress tracking and course correction processes
- Case studies of successful strategies

## Non-Functional Requirements

### NFR-1: Quality Standards
- All strategies based on proven business frameworks
- Content follows strategic thinking best practices
- Templates are professionally designed
- Examples from successful startups and established businesses

### NFR-2: Accessibility
- Simple language for non-strategy experts
- Actionable steps without excessive jargon
- Visual aids and examples included
- Checklist-based implementation

### NFR-3: Integration
- Compatible with research integration skill
- Works with common strategy tools
- Provides tool recommendations
- Includes setup guides

### NFR-4: Scalability
- Strategies work for B2B and B2C
- Applicable to various business models
- Adaptable to different stages of growth
- Market-size appropriate

## Constraints

### Technical Constraints
- Works with standard documentation tools
- Compatible with major platforms
- Supports common CMS and collaboration tools

### Business Constraints
- Focuses on strategic thinking, not day-to-day operations
- Results depend on product-market fit and execution
- Requires honest self-assessment from users

### Dependencies
- Requires research integration skill for industry insights
- Works with product builder for product strategy
- Complements marketing growth skill for go-to-market

## Assumptions
- Users have a product or business concept
- Users have basic understanding of their market
- Users are willing to invest time in strategic thinking
- Users have access to market research data sources

## Acceptance Criteria

### AC-1: Business Model Design
- **Given**: User describes their product and value proposition
- **When**: User requests business model design
- **Then**: Skill provides complete business model canvas with alternatives
- **Verification**: `human-judgment` - Framework is comprehensive and actionable

### AC-2: Competitive Strategy
- **Given**: User provides competitive landscape information
- **When**: User requests competitive strategy
- **Then**: Skill provides competitive positioning and response playbooks
- **Verification**: `human-judgment` - Strategy is differentiated and realistic

### AC-3: Market Analysis
- **Given**: User provides market context
- **When**: User requests market analysis
- **Then**: Skill provides TAM/SAM/SOM calculations and trend analysis
- **Verification**: `programmatic` - Analysis follows established methodologies

### AC-4: Strategic Planning
- **Given**: User has a business concept
- **When**: User requests strategic plan
- **Then**: Skill provides SWOT analysis and actionable roadmap
- **Verification**: `human-judgment` - Plan is realistic and prioritized

### AC-5: Execution Playbooks
- **Given**: User has a strategy selected
- **When**: User requests implementation guidance
- **Then**: Skill provides playbooks with checklists and milestones
- **Verification**: `programmatic` - Playbooks follow structure guidelines
