# Science-Philosophy Connection Pattern

## Core Insight
Science provides methods, philosophy provides perspectives; the combination produces profound insights.

## Classic Connection Patterns

### 1. Scientific Methodology → Philosophical Thinking
#### Connection Points
- **Positivism** → **Empirical Verification**
- **Falsifiability** → **Skepticism**
- **Paradigm Shift** → **Thinking Revolution**

#### Applications
- Use scientific methods to verify philosophical hypotheses
- Use philosophical perspectives to interpret scientific discoveries
- Break down disciplinary barriers for cross-disciplinary thinking

---

### 2. Specific Sciences → Philosophical Branches
| Science Field | Philosophical Branch | Connection Theme |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Physics | Metaphysics | Time, space, causality |
| Biology | Ethics | Bioethics, gene editing |
| Cognitive Science | Philosophy of Mind | Consciousness, free will |
| Computer Science | Logic | Algorithms, artificial intelligence |
| Social Science | Political Philosophy | Justice, fairness, power |

---

### 3. Philosophical Methods → Scientific Research
#### Scientific Applications of Philosophical Tools
- **Logical Analysis** → Rigor of theory construction
- **Thought Experiments** → Thinking style for hypothesis testing
- **Concept Clarification** → Precision of terminology definitions
- **Critical Thinking** → Spirit of peer review

#### Examples
- Thought experiment: Einstein's thought experiment of chasing light
- Concept clarification: What is "consciousness"?
- Critical thinking: Questioning the premises of research hypotheses

---

## Cross-Domain Mental Models

### Scientific Thinking → Everyday Decisions
| Scientific Principle | Decision Application |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| Controlled Experiment | A/B testing decisions |
| Probabilistic Thinking | Judgments under uncertainty |
| Falsification Attitude | Looking for opposing evidence |

### Philosophical Thinking → Scientific Research
| Philosophical Concept | Research Application |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| Ontology | Studying what exists |
| Epistemology | How to acquire knowledge |
| Axiology | Value judgments of research |
