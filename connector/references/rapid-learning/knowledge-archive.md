# Knowledge Archive System

## Purpose

Systematically build and maintain cross-domain knowledge.

## Theory Base

- **Spaced Repetition**: Long-term retention
- **Associative Learning**: Strengthen connections
- **Generation Effect**: Active recall

## Core Principle

> "Build your second brain. Connect across domains."

### Example Card

```markdown
# Economics: Price Signals

**Date**: 2026-05-20
**Source**: Read "The Undercover Economist"
**Confidence**: 4

## The Idea

Prices communicate information about supply and demand 
across millions of people who never talk to each other.

## Explanation

When a resource becomes scarce, its price rises. This 
signal motivates people to: (1) use less, (2) find 
substitutes, (3) increase supply. All without central 
coordination.

## Analogy

Prices are like a weather forecast for the economy—
they tell you what's coming without explaining why.

## Connection to Existing Knowledge

This connects to:
- Systems thinking (feedback loops)
- Blockchain (prices as consensus)
- Marketing (value communication)

## When to Use

- Evaluating business decisions
- Understanding market failures
- Explaining why things cost what they do

## Review Log

| Date | Ease | Next Review |
|------|------|-------------|
| May 20 | 4 | May 24 |
```


### Connection Example

```markdown
# Connection: Evolution ↔ Business Strategy

**Created**: 2026-05-20
**Strength**: 5

## The Bridge

Both involve adaptation to changing environments through 
variation and selection.

## How They're Similar

1. Both have variation: Genetic/strategic diversity
2. Both have selection: Survival of fittest/market selection
3. Both have inheritance: Successful traits spread

## How They're Different

1. Evolution is slow (generations), business can adapt faster
2. Evolution has no foresight, business can anticipate
3. Evolution optimizes for survival, business optimizes for growth

## What Transfers

The concept of "adaptive radiation"—when new opportunities 
emerge, successful entities diversify into niches.

**Application**: When entering new markets, consider whether 
you're competing on existing dimensions or creating new ones.

## Real Example

Amazon's expansion from books to everything echoes how early 
mammals diversified after dinosaurs died.
```

## Spaced Review System

### Review Schedule

```markdown
## Review Schedule

Day 1: Learn → Create card → First review
Day 2: Light review (5 min)
Day 4: Recall + Check connections
Day 7: Apply in context
Day 14: Connect to new learning
Day 30: Major synthesis
Day 60: Expert level review
```

### Review Process

```markdown
## Daily Review (10 min)

1. Pick 5 cards at random
2. For each:
   - Read front only
   - Try to recall from memory
   - Check back
   - Rate ease (1-5)
3. Update next review date

## Weekly Connection (30 min)

1. Review this week's new cards
2. Find 1 connection to existing knowledge
3. Write connection note
4. Update network diagram
```

## Application Workflow

### When Learning Something New

```
1. QuickScan → Get overview
2. Connection Discovery → Find links
3. Create Cards → Document knowledge
4. Update Network → Visualize connections
5. Schedule Review → Space it out
```

### When Facing a Problem

```
1. State the problem
2. Search knowledge network for relevant concepts
3. Find connections across domains
4. Generate novel approaches
5. Apply and learn
```
