# Feynman Technique Application

## Methodology Description
Learning method named after physicist Feynman, core is "teaching is learning".

## Feynman Technique 4 Steps

### Step 1: Choose a Topic
- Choose the concept you want to learn
- Write the title at the top of paper or document

**Key Points**:
- Start with a small, specific concept
- Don't bite off too much at first

---

### Step 2: Teach to a Child
- Imagine you're teaching this concept to a child
- Explain in the simplest language
- Avoid jargon

**Scientific Principles**:
- Knowledge Curse: Experts have trouble understanding beginner perspective
- This step forces you to find gaps in your understanding

**Tips**:
- If you get stuck, go back and re-learn
- Use analogies and stories to help explain
- Ask yourself "Why is this so?"

---

### Step 3: Fill Gaps
- Find places where you had difficulty explaining
- Go back to materials, re-learn
- Until you can smoothly explain the entire concept

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Can I explain why?
- [ ] Can I give examples?
- [ ] Do I know the limitations?
- [ ] Do I know when to apply it?

---

### Step 4: Simplify & Analogize
- Organize your explanation
- Delete unnecessary complexity
- Use better analogies to make it more understandable

**Simplification Standards**:
- Understandable without a dictionary
- A 10-year-old can get the gist
- Can summarize core in one sentence

---

## Feynman Technique Variations

### Variation 1: Write an Explanation
- Explain in blog post style
- Pretend to write for someone who knows nothing
- Publish to get feedback

---

### Variation 2: Video Explanation
- Record yourself explaining
- Watch back to find problems
- Upload and share (optional)

---

### Variation 3: Group Teaching
- Find a few friends
- Take turns explaining
- Ask questions and give feedback to each other

---

## Feynman Technique Template

```
Concept:
Target Audience: 10-year-old child

1st Explanation:
(Write your explanation)

Problems Found:
(List where you got stuck)

Re-Learning Notes:
(Supplement your knowledge)

2nd Simplified Explanation:
(Simpler version)

Analogy/Story:
(Analogy to help understand)

One-Sentence Summary:
(Most core content)
```

## Common Mistakes

❌ Using jargon and complex terms
❌ Skipping places you don't understand
❌ Only thinking in your head, not saying out loud
❌ Not seeking feedback

✅ Using simplest language
✅ Facing knowledge gaps
✅ Speaking or writing out
✅ Accepting challenges from others
