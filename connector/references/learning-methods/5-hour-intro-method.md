# 5-Hour Intro Method

## Methodology Description
Based on cognitive load theory and spaced repetition, quickly master core knowledge of a new domain in 5 hours.

## 5-Hour Learning Plan

### Hour 1: Panoramic Map
- **Goal**: Establish global cognition
- **Activities**:
  - Read 3 introduction articles from different angles
  - Watch 2 introductory videos
  - Draw a preliminary concept map

**Scientific Principle**: Build a framework first, then fill in details, reducing cognitive load

---

### Hour 2: Core Concepts
- **Goal**: Understand the most critical 3-5 concepts
- **Activities**:
  - Find high-frequency terms in the domain
  - Explain each concept in your own words
  - Create concept cards

**Scientific Principle**: Generation effect - active learning creates deeper memories than passive reading

---

### Hour 3: Practical Application
- **Goal**: Hands-on, get intuitive experience
- **Activities**:
  - Complete the simplest tutorial
  - Run Hello World example
  - Try modifying parameters to observe results

**Scientific Principle**: Situated learning - learn by doing

---

### Hour 4: Connection Discovery
- **Goal**: Build connections with existing knowledge
- **Activities**:
  - Find 3 similarities to domains you already know
  - Think about how to apply to your work
  - Record cross-domain insights

**Scientific Principle**: Transfer learning - use existing knowledge to accelerate understanding

---

### Hour 5: Consolidation & Review
- **Goal**: Strengthen memory, plan next steps
- **Activities**:
  - Review notes from previous 4 hours
  - Explain using Feynman Technique to someone
  - Develop next-step learning plan

**Scientific Principle**: Spaced repetition, testing effect

---

## Hourly Breakdown (50 min learning + 10 min break)

### 50-Minute Learning Time
- 25 minutes deep focus
- 5 minutes quick review
- 20 minutes practice or connection
- 5 minutes break (walk around, drink water)

### 10-Minute Break
- Leave the screen
- Stretch your body
- Let your brain organize information

## Key Principles

1. **Don't chase perfection**: 5 hours is just an intro
2. **Active participation**: Output while learning
3. **Build connections**: Link to known knowledge
4. **Review promptly**: Review cards at intervals
5. **Practice first**: Hands-on is more important than just watching
