# Cognitive Science Research Papers - Literature Review

## Executive Summary

This document compiles key cognitive science research papers that form the theoretical foundation for the **Connector Skill**. These papers inform the design of rapid learning methodologies for cross-domain knowledge acquisition.

---

## 1. Foundational Memory and Learning Theories

### 1.1 Spaced Repetition and Memory

**Ebbinghaus, H. (1885)**
*Memory: A Contribution to Experimental Psychology*
- **Key Contribution**: Forgetting Curve - first systematic measurement of memory decay
- **Core Finding**: Information decays exponentially; review at optimal intervals prevents forgetting
- **Application**: Spaced repetition algorithms, review scheduling in Knowledge Archive

**Wozniak, P. (1990s)**
*SuperMemo and the SM-2 Algorithm*
- **Key Contribution**: Practical implementation of spaced repetition
- **Core Finding**: Optimal review intervals increase exponentially with successful recall
- **Application**: Anki-style flashcards, Knowledge Archive's review reminder system

### 1.2 Generation Effect

**Slamecka, N. J., & Graf, P. (1978)**
"The Generation Effect"
*Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Learning and Memory, 4*(6), 592-604
- **Key Contribution**: Active generation enhances memory more than passive reading
- **Core Finding**: Self-generated information is remembered better than information read
- **Application**: Active recall exercises, fill-in-the-blank learning, Connection Discovery prompts

### 1.3 Cognitive Load Theory

**Sweller, J. (1988)**
"Cognitive Load During Problem Solving"
*Cognitive Science, 12*(2), 257-285
- **Key Contribution**: Three types of cognitive load (intrinsic, extraneous, germane)
- **Core Finding**: Working memory is limited; instructional design can reduce unnecessary load
- **Application**: Layered information delivery, concept tree chunking, Progressive Disclosure

**Sweller, J. (2011)**
"Cognitive Load Theory"
In J. Mestre & B. Ross (Eds.), *The Psychology of Learning and Motivation*
- **Key Contribution**: Updated framework with evolutionary perspective
- **Core Finding**: Cognitive load principles apply to real-world problem solving
- **Application**: QuickScan's 30-minute cognitive map, mastery path layering

## 2. Learning and Expertise Development

### 2.1 Situated Learning

**Lave, J., & Wenger, E. (1991)**
*Situated Learning: Legitimate Peripheral Participation*
Cambridge University Press
- **Key Contribution**: Learning is context-dependent and social
- **Core Finding**: Knowledge cannot be separated from its practice context
- **Application**: Real-world project scenarios in learning paths, expert conversation preparation

### 2.2 Transfer of Learning

**Thorndike, E. L., & Woodworth, R. S. (1901)**
"The Influence of Improvement in One Mental Function"
*Psychological Review, 8*(3), 247-261
- **Key Contribution**: First systematic study of learning transfer
- **Core Finding**: Transfer depends on identical elements; deep structures matter more

**Salomon, G., & Perkins, D. N. (1989)**
"Transfer of Cognitive Skills"
In L. Resnick & L. Klopfer (Eds.), *Toward the Thinking Curriculum*
- **Key Contribution**: Near vs. far transfer distinction
- **Core Finding**: Far transfer requires explicit instruction in principles
- **Application**: Connection Discovery's deep structure analysis

### 2.3 Desirable Difficulties

**Bjork, R. A. (1994)**
"Institutional Impediments to Effective Training"
In J. R. Anderson & R. Lewin (Eds.), *Learning and Skills*
- **Key Contribution**: "Desirable difficulties" enhance long-term learning
- **Core Finding**: Making learning harder in the short term improves long-term retention

**Bjork, E. L., & Bjork, R. A. (2011)**
"Making Things Hard on Yourself, But in a Good Way"
In A. Benjamin (Ed.), *Successful Remembering and Successful Forgetting*
- **Key Contribution**: Practical implications of desirable difficulties
- **Core Finding**: Spacing, interleaving, and retrieval practice are beneficial
- **Application**: Active recall design, interleaved practice in mastery paths

## 3. Metacognition and Self-Regulation

### 3.1 Metacognition

**Flavell, J. H. (1979)**
"Metacognition and Cognitive Monitoring"
*American Psychologist, 34*(10), 906-911
- **Key Contribution**: First formal definition of metacognition
- **Core Finding**: Learners need to monitor and regulate their own cognition
- **Application**: Self-assessment checklists, learning path reflection points

**Schraw, G., & Dennison, R. S. (1994)**
"Assessing Metacognitive Awareness"
*Contemporary Educational Psychology, 19*(4), 460-475
- **Key Contribution**: Assessment tools for metacognitive knowledge
- **Core Finding**: Metacognitive skills can be measured and taught
- **Application**: QuickScan's assessment phase, mastery path checkpoints

---

## 4. Knowledge and Communication

### 4.1 Curse of Knowledge

**Camerer, C., Loewenstein, G., & Weber, M. (1989)**
"The Curse of Knowledge in Economic Settings"
*Journal of Political Economy, 97*(5), 1232-1254
- **Key Contribution**: Experts cannot easily imagine novice perspectives
- **Core Finding**: Knowledge makes it harder to communicate with novices
- **Application**: Plain English explanations, analogies in QuickScan, Expert Talk Prep

### 4.2 Expertise and Knowledge Organization

**Chi, M. T. H., Feltovich, P. J., & Glaser, R. (1981)**
"Categorization and Representation of Physics Problems"
*Cognitive Science, 5*(2), 121-152
- **Key Contribution**: Experts organize knowledge by deep principles, not surface features
- **Core Finding**: Novices focus on surface similarity; experts see structural similarities
- **Application**: Deep structure analysis in Connection Discovery

---

## 5. Adaptive Learning and Memory Systems

### 5.1 Testing Effect

**Roediger, H. L., & Karpicke, J. D. (2006)**
"Test-Enhanced Learning"
*Psychological Science, 17*(3), 249-255
- **Key Contribution**: Retrieval practice is more effective than restudying
- **Core Finding**: Taking tests improves later retention more than spending equivalent time reading
- **Application**: Active recall in Knowledge Archive, mastery path assessments

### 5.2 Interleaving and Mixed Practice

**Rohrer, D., Dedrick, R. F., & Stencil, S. (2015)**
"Interleaving Helps Students Distinguish Among Similar Concepts"
*Experimental Psychology, 62*(6), 385-392
- **Key Contribution**: Mixing different problem types improves discrimination
- **Core Finding**: Interleaved practice, though harder initially, leads to better long-term retention
- **Application**: Mixed practice in mastery paths, cross-domain connection exercises

---

## 6. Cross-Domain Learning and Innovation

### 6.1 Analogical Reasoning

**Gentner, D. (1983)**
"Structure-Mapping: A Theoretical Framework for Analogy"
*Cognitive Science, 7*(2), 155-170
- **Key Contribution**: Structure-mapping theory of analogy
- **Core Finding**: Analogies are perceived as system correspondences, not surface similarities
- **Application**: Connection Discovery's cross-domain analogy framework

### 6.2 Cognitive Networks and Knowledge Structure

**De Deyne, S., et al. (2019)**
"The Small World of Words"
*Psychological Science, 30*(7), 958-966
- **Key Contribution**: Human semantic network structure mapped
- **Core Finding**: Words are organized by semantic similarity and co-occurrence
- **Application**: Knowledge graph visualization, connection strength algorithms

---

## 7. Dual Coding and Multimedia Learning

### 7.1 Dual Coding Theory

**Paivio, A. (1971)**
*Mental Imagery in Abstract Thinking*
Prentice-Hall
- **Key Contribution**: Dual coding - verbal and visual channels
- **Core Finding**: Information coded in both modalities is better remembered
- **Application**: Concept trees, visual diagrams in QuickScan, multimodal explanations

### 7.2 Multimedia Learning

**Mayer, R. E. (2001)**
*Multimedia Learning*
Cambridge University Press
- **Key Contribution**: Principles for effective multimedia learning
- **Core Finding**: Spatial contiguity, redundancy, coherence principles
- **Application**: Design guidelines for learning path materials

---

## 8. Practical Implementation Research

### 8.1 Spaced Repetition in Practice

**Settledge, R. (2015)**
"The Science of Brilliant Memory"
In M. Cepeda et al. (Eds.), *Spaced Retrieval and Optimized Interleaving*
- **Key Contribution**: Optimal spacing intervals for long-term retention
- **Application**: Knowledge Archive's review scheduling algorithm

### 8.2 Rapid Skill Acquisition

**Dreyfus, H. L., & Dreyfus, S. E. (1986)**
*Mind Over Machine*
Free Press
- **Key Contribution**: Five-stage model from novice to expert
- **Core Finding**: Intuition develops through extensive practice and pattern recognition
- **Application**: Mastery Path's Surface/Middle/Deep layer framework

|--------|-------------|---------------------|

| Generation Effect | Active > passive | Active recall, fill-in exercises |

| Situated Learning | Context matters | Real-world project scenarios |
| Transfer Learning | Deep > surface | Cross-domain connection discovery |
| Desirable Difficulties | Harder now = better later | Spaced testing, interleaving |
