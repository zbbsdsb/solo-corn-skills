# Mental Models Quick Reference

A condensed one-page reference for all 45 mental models organized by domain.

---

## Decision & Reasoning Models

| # | Model | Core Concept | Key Question | Primary Use |
|---|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Bayesian Thinking** | Update beliefs based on new evidence with calibrated probability | "How much should this evidence change my belief?" | Evaluating information, making predictions |
| 2 | **Circle of Competence** | Know the boundaries of your true expertise | "What do I actually understand well?" | Avoiding overconfidence, focusing effort |
| 3 | **Confirmation Bias Awareness** | Actively seek disconfirming evidence | "What would prove me wrong?" | Reducing self-deception, improving analysis |
| 4 | **First Principles** | Break problems to fundamental truths | "What do we know is undeniably true?" | Innovation, problem-solving novel challenges |
| 5 | **Hanlon's Razor** | Assume incompetence before malice | "Could this be an honest mistake?" | Reducing unnecessary conflict |
| 6 | **Inversion** | Think backward from failure to avoid it | "How would we definitely fail?" | Risk mitigation, strategic planning |
| 7 | **Occam's Razor** | Prefer the simplest explanation | "What's the simplest explanation?" | Quick diagnosis, avoiding overthinking |
| 8 | **Regression to Mean** | Extreme outcomes naturally move toward average | "Is this just normal fluctuation?" | Realistic expectations, avoiding overreaction |
| 9 | **Second-Order Thinking** | Consider consequences of consequences | "And then what happens?" | Strategic decisions, unintended consequences |
| 10 | **Survivorship Bias Awareness** | Study failures, not just successes | "What don't we see?" | Learning from mistakes, realistic analysis |

## Execution & Learning Models

| # | Model | Core Concept | Key Question | Primary Use |
|---|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| 11 | **Compound Growth** | Small improvements accumulate exponentially | "Are we reinvesting in improvement?" | Long-term planning, habits |
| 12 | **Kaizen** | Continuous small improvements | "How can we get 1% better?" | Process improvement, personal development |
| 13 | **Learning Curve** | Performance improves with experience | "How much faster will we get?" | Project planning, expectations |
| 14 | **Margins of Safety** | Build buffer against uncertainty | "What could go wrong?" | Risk management, investing |
| 15 | **OODA Loop** | Observe-Orient-Decide-Act cycle | "Can we iterate faster than changes?" | Rapid decision-making, military strategy |
| 16 | **Optionality** | Preserve ability to make future choices | "Are we keeping our options open?" | Strategy under uncertainty |
| 17 | **PDCA Cycle** | Plan-Do-Check-Act iterative improvement | "Did our change work?" | Process improvement, quality control |
| 18 | **Two Boxes Thinking** | Separate controllable from uncontrollable | "What can we actually control?" | Focus, reducing anxiety about external factors |

## Psychology & Behavior Models

| # | Model | Core Concept | Key Question | Primary Use |
|---|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| 19 | **Anchoring Bias** | First information disproportionately influences | "What reference point is skewing me?" | Negotiation, decision-making |
| 20 | **Authority Bias** | Overweight opinions of perceived experts | "Is this expert in their domain?" | Evaluating advice, avoiding manipulation |
| 21 | **Availability Heuristic** | Judge likelihood by ease of recall | "Am I confusing familiar with likely?" | Risk assessment, planning |
| 22 | **Cognitive Biases Overview** | Systematic thinking errors to watch for | "What mental shortcuts are misleading me?" | Self-awareness, critical thinking |
| 23 | **Commitment Consistency** | People follow through on prior commitments | "What small commitments lead to bigger ones?" | Influence, habit formation |
| 24 | **Incentive Structures** | Behavior follows rewards and punishments | "What's actually being incentivized?" | Design systems, understanding behavior |
| 25 | **Loss Aversion** | Losses hurt more than equivalent gains | "Am I avoiding necessary losses?" | Risk decisions, negotiation |
| 26 | **Scarcity Effect** | Rare items perceived as more valuable | "Is exclusivity helping or hurting?" | Pricing, urgency creation |
| 27 | **Social Proof** | People follow others' behavior | "What are others actually doing?" | Marketing, influence |

## Essential Model Pairs

Some problems are best addressed by combining complementary models:

| Pair | Combined Use |
|------|-------------|
| **Occam's Razor + First Principles** | Balance simplicity with depth |
| **Inversion + Second-Order Thinking** | Comprehensive risk analysis |
| **Circle of Competence + Margins of Safety** | Confident but careful decision-making |
| **Feedback Loops + Leverage Points** | Effective system intervention |
| **Regression to Mean + Bayesian Thinking** | Calibrated probability updates |
| **Loss Aversion + Incentive Structures** | Understanding behavioral economics |
| **Survivorship Bias + First Principles** | Learning from failure |
| **OODA Loop + PDCA Cycle** | Rapid and thorough improvement |

## Quick Reference: Decision Tree

```
START: What are you trying to do?
│
├─ UNDERSTAND a situation
│  ├─ Why did it happen? → Hanlon's Razor / Second-Order Thinking
│  ├─ How does it work? → Feedback Loops / Emergence
│  └─ What might happen? → Bayesian Thinking / Regression to Mean
│
├─ DECIDE on action
│  ├─ Simple choice? → Occam's Razor / Two Boxes Thinking
│  ├─ Complex choice? → First Principles / Inversion
│  └─ Under uncertainty? → Optionality / Margins of Safety
│
├─ INFLUENCE behavior
│  ├─ Individual? → Incentive Structures / Loss Aversion
│  ├─ Group? → Social Proof / Commitment Consistency
│  └─ Organization? → Leverage Points / Feedback Loops
│
├─ COMPETE strategically
│  ├─ Market analysis? → Porter's Five Forces / SWOT
│  ├─ Positioning? → Competitive Moats / Niche Dominance
│  └─ Negotiation? → Bargaining Power / Game Theory
│
└─ IMPROVE continuously
   ├─ Fast iteration? → OODA Loop
   ├─ Systematic? → PDCA Cycle / Kaizen
   └─ Long-term? → Compound Growth / Learning Curve
```
