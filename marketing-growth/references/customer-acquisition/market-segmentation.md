# Customer Acquisition Framework

## 1. Market Segmentation

### 1.1 Segmentation Criteria

**Demographic Segmentation:**
- Age range
- Gender
- Income level
- Education
- Occupation
- Location/Geography

**Behavioral Segmentation:**
- Purchase behavior
- Usage rate
- Brand loyalty
- Benefits sought
- User status (new, potential, lapsed)

**Psychographic Segmentation:**
- Lifestyle
- Values and beliefs
- Interests and hobbies
- Personality traits
- Life goals

### 2.2 Example Persona

```
Customer Persona: Busy Professional Sarah
========================================

Photo/Avatar: Professional woman in 30s with laptop
Age: 32
Location: San Francisco, CA
Occupation: Marketing Manager
Income: $85,000/year

Background:
Sarah manages marketing at a mid-sized tech company. She's responsible for project outcomes but has limited time to manage complex tools. She values efficiency and is always looking for ways to streamline her workflow.

Goals:
- Complete projects on time
- Reduce time spent on coordination
- Improve team visibility
- Get better reporting insights

Challenges/Pains:
- Too many meetings to discuss status updates
- No real-time visibility into project progress
- Difficult to track deliverables across teams
- Manual status reports take too much time

Motivations:
- Career advancement
- Team success
- Work-life balance
- Professional development

Preferred Channels:
- LinkedIn for professional content
- Email for work communications
- Podcasts for learning
- Slack for quick updates

Content Preferences:
- Case studies
- Quick tips
- Video tutorials
- Industry reports

Buying Behavior:
- Researches independently first
- Asks colleagues for recommendations
- Needs management approval for >$500
- Willing to try free trials

Quote:
"I don't have time for tools that create more work than they save."
```

```

□ Messaging matches customer language
```

## 5. Acquisition Channel Selection

### 5.1 Channel Evaluation Matrix

| Channel | Reach | Cost | Time to Results | Scalability | Score |
|---------|-------|------|-----------------|-------------|-------|
| Content/SEO | High | Low | 6-12 months | High | |
| Social Media | High | Low-Medium | 1-3 months | Medium | |
| Email Marketing | Medium | Low | 1-2 months | High | |
| Paid Search | Medium | Medium-High | Immediate | Medium | |
| Paid Social | High | Medium-High | Immediate | High | |
| Partnerships | Medium | Low-Medium | 1-3 months | Medium | |
| PR/Media | High | Variable | 1-6 months | Low | |
| Community | Medium | Low | 3-6 months | High | |

### 5.2 Channel Selection Criteria

**For Solo Entrepreneurs:**

Priority Channels (Low cost, High impact):
1. Content Marketing + SEO
2. Email Marketing
3. Social Media (1-2 platforms)
4. Strategic Partnerships
5. Community Building

Secondary Channels (Higher investment):
1. Paid Search (Google Ads)
2. Paid Social (LinkedIn/Facebook)
3. Influencer Marketing
4. PR and Media

### 5.3 Channel Prioritization Framework

```
Step 1: List all potential channels
Step 2: Score each channel (1-5) for:
  - Cost effectiveness
  - Time to results
  - Scalability
  - Strategic fit
Step 3: Calculate weighted score
Step 4: Prioritize top 3 channels
Step 5: Create channel-specific strategies
```
