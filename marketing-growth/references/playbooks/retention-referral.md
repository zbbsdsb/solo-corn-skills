# Marketing Playbooks

## Retention and Referral

### 1. Playbook Overview

```
Playbook: Customer Retention and Referral
========================================

Objective: Maximize customer lifetime value through retention and advocacy
Timeline: 90-day foundation + continuous optimization
Focus: Long-term customer success, loyalty, and word-of-mouth growth

Core Principles:
- Retention is cheaper than acquisition (5x cheaper on average)
- Happy customers are your best marketers
- Every touchpoint is an opportunity to delight
- Data-driven personalization wins

Target Outcomes:
- Monthly churn: <2% (B2B) or <5% (B2C)
- Net Revenue Retention: >100%
- NPS Score: >50
- Referral rate: >20% of new customers from referrals
- Customer LTV: 3x+ CAC
```

### 3. NPS Improvement Framework

#### 3.1 NPS Fundamentals

```
NET PROMOTER SCORE FRAMEWORK
=============================

NPS CALCULATION
---------------

Survey Question: "How likely are you to recommend [Product] to a friend or colleague?"

Scale: 0-10

Promoters (9-10): Loyal enthusiasts who will keep buying
Passives (7-8): Satisfied but unenthusiastic customers
Detractors (0-6): Unhappy customers who can damage brand

Formula: % Promoters - % Detractors = NPS

Benchmark Goals:
- Below 0: Poor
- 0-30: Good
- 30-50: Great
- 50-70: Excellent
- 70+: World-class


NPS COLLECTION STRATEGY
------------------------

Touchpoints:
□ Post-onboarding (Day 7)
□ Post-milestone (feature adoption)
□ Post-support interaction
□ Quarterly business review
□ Annual renewal
□ Churn moment

Timing:
□ Wait until value is demonstrated
□ Don't interrupt critical workflows
□ Follow up detractors quickly

Sampling:
□ Target: 30%+ response rate
□ Minimum: 10 responses/month
□ Segment by plan/t tenure/segment
```

### 8. Common Mistakes and Best Practices

```
RETENTION PITFALLS TO AVOID
============================

Strategy:
✗ Treating all customers the same
✗ Waiting for customers to complain
✗ Over-focusing on acquisition
✗ Ignoring early warning signs
✗ No defined success criteria

Execution:
✗ Slow response to issues
✗ Poor handoffs between teams
✗ One-size-fits-all onboarding
✗ No follow-up after calls
✗ Ignoring customer feedback

Measurement:
✗ Tracking vanity metrics only
✗ No cohort analysis
✗ No health scoring
✗ No regular reviews
✗ Not tying to revenue

Referral:
✗ Making program too complicated
✗ Poor tracking/fraud
✗ Ignoring referrer experience
✗ One-time only approach
✗ No recognition/celebration


RETENTION BEST PRACTICES
=========================

Proactive:
✓ Monitor health scores daily
✓ Reach out before problems arise
✓ Celebrate customer wins
✓ Personalize every interaction
✓ Build relationships at all levels

Systematic:
✓ Automate onboarding
✓ Create escalation paths
✓ Measure everything
✓ Regular process reviews
✓ Document learnings

Customer-Centric:
✓ Focus on success, not sales
✓ Be honest about limitations
✓ Go above and beyond
✓ Show genuine appreciation
✓ Treat them as partners


REFERRAL PROGRAM BEST PRACTICES
================================

Design:
✓ Two-sided rewards perform better
✓ Make sharing effortless
✓ Track accurately and transparently
✓ Have clear terms
✓ Keep rewards compelling

Promotion:
✓ Ask at success moments
✓ Don't over-promote
✓ Personalize messaging
✓ Leverage existing advocates
✓ Create urgency

Recognition:
✓ Thank personally
✓ Public recognition
✓ Exclusive experiences
✓ Career value for advocates
✓ Make them feel special


KEY TAKEAWAYS
=============

1. Retention is 5x cheaper than acquisition
   - Invest in keeping customers happy

2. Measure what matters
   - Churn, NPS, LTV, referrals

3. Be proactive, not reactive
   - Reach out before problems escalate

   - NPS 9-10 are your best marketers

   - Treat customers as individuals

   - Optimize based on data
```
