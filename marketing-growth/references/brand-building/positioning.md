# Brand Building Framework

## 1. Brand Positioning

### 1.1 Brand Positioning Statement

```
Brand Positioning Template
==========================

For [TARGET AUDIENCE]
who [PROBLEM/NEED],
[BRAND NAME] is the [CATEGORY/SOLUTION]
that [KEY BENEFIT].
Unlike [COMPETITORS],
[BRAND] [PRIMARY DIFFERENTIATOR].


Example:
For busy marketing managers
who struggle with project coordination,
TaskFlow is the project management tool
that eliminates status meetings.
Unlike traditional project tools,
TaskFlow provides real-time visibility
without adding administrative overhead.
```

## 3. Brand Voice Guidelines

### 3.1 Voice Characteristics

```
Brand Voice Spectrum
====================

FUN & PLAYFUL ◀━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━▶ SERIOUS & PROFESSIONAL
     ○          │                   │           ○
                │    Current       │
                │   Position ─────┼──→ [You are here]
                │                   │
TALK LIKE ◀━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━▶ TALK AT ME
 FRIENDS                              DON'T TALK TO ME
     ○                                   ○

Voice Dimensions:
1. FORMALITY: Formal ←──────────→ Casual
2. TONE: Serious ←───────────────→ Humorous
3. EXPERTISE: Expert ←───────────→ Novice
4. EMOTION: Cool ←───────────────→ Warm
```

### 3.2 Voice Dos and Don'ts

```
Brand Voice Guidelines
======================

DO USE:
---------
✓ [Word/tone 1] - Example: "Streamline" when talking about efficiency
✓ [Word/tone 2] - Example: "Your team deserves" when showing care
✓ [Word/tone 3] - Example: Short, punchy sentences

DON'T USE:
-----------
✗ [Word/tone 1] - Instead of: "Utilize" use: "Use"
✗ [Word/tone 2] - Instead of: "Synergy" use: "Working together"
✗ [Word/tone 3] - Instead of: Passive voice, use: Active voice


EXAMPLE COPY:

Headline (Do): "Save 10 Hours Every Week"
Headline (Don't): "Enhance Your Productivity Metrics"

Body (Do): "You work hard. Your tools should work harder."
Body (Don't): "Our revolutionary solution leverages cutting-edge technology."

CTA (Do): "Start Free Trial"
CTA (Don't): "Initiate Your Free Trial Experience"
```

### 5.6 PR Outreach Best Practices

```
DO:
-----
✓ Personalize every email with specific reasons why that
  journalist/publication is a good fit
✓ Lead with the story angle, not the product
✓ Offer exclusive access or data when possible
✓ Follow up 2-3 times over 2-3 weeks
✓ Time outreach for industry news cycles
✓ Build relationships before pitching
✓ Provide all assets ready to use
✓ Be responsive to journalist requests

DON'T:
------
✗ Send mass generic pitches
✗ Overhype or exaggerate claims
✗ Ask the journalist to "let me know if you're interested"
✗ Send attachments without prior permission
✗ Follow up hourly or daily
✗ Pitch products that aren't relevant to their beat
✗ Ignore their editorial calendar
✗ Get defensive if rejected

FOLLOW-UP TEMPLATE:

Subject: Re: [Original Subject] - Quick follow-up

Hi [Name],

Just wanted to bump this to the top of your inbox.
[One sentence reminder of the story angle].

I'm also happy to provide any additional information or
schedule a quick call if that's easier.

Either way, no pressure - just wanted to make sure you
saw this before [relevant deadline].

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```
